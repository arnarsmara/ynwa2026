The Albert Docks
- Albert Dock is arguably the heart of Liverpool. Since its opening in 1846, the Albert Docks have undergone numerous transformations and developments to become what it is today. From its role during WWII, up until its UNESCO World Heritage Site recognition in 2004, the docks are full of history. We were spoilt for choice for places to eat, drink, visit and stay in this area. Smuggler's Cove and Revolucion de Cuba are two great bars/restaurants worth checking out. Many of Liverpool's best museums and galleries are found in this area too, including the Tate, the International Slavery Museum and Museum of Liverpool.

Ohannes Burger Liverpool &Town Uber&Deliveroo&Just Eat
- Ohannes is a small chain of burger restaurants in Liverpool, and the one on Bold Street is the best. The burgers are delicious, and the menu has a good variety of options. The restaurant has a great atmosphere, and the staff are friendly and attentive. It's a great place to grab a quick bite to eat while exploring the city.

Concert Square & Baltic Triangle
- Concert Square is the main nightlife area in Liverpool, and it's where you'll find most of the city's bars and clubs. It's a great place to go out for a night on the town, with a lively atmosphere and plenty of options for entertainment. The Baltic Triangle is another popular nightlife area in Liverpool, known for its alternative and indie music scene. It's a great place to discover new music and enjoy a night out with friends.
